Branding Basics

What does it offer?

Our [ICON] is an online retailer which sells products that focus on the modern industrial design of everyday items. We emphasise quality of the product through material choice, using titanium brass, aluminium, high quality plastics and synthetics. Specialising in computer keyboards, they offer a wide variety of styles and options which all provide a premium experience. Along with keyboards, premium stationery and everyday carry items (pens, cutting utensils, tools) are offered with the same expectation of quality and clean minimal design language.

Who is [ICON] to potential customers?

[ICON] has a friendly yet professional demeanour but focuses more on product design, drawing customers in through distinct styling rather than a friendly personality. They wish for the customer to fall in love with the work and connect with the product rather than the company as a focus.

The target audience for these items are hobbyists, (people who have many different hobbies within a scope) minimalists, and industrial designers. All of which have an affinity for “form and function” goods which are not only functional but overengineered to provide both function and form.

  • limited experience with mechanical keyboards, but understands the fundamentals
  • Appreciates bespoke items and niche tools to help with everyday life

Why might someone to purchase a product from [ICON]

[ICON] relies on the unique clean and industrial styling of its products to entice someone to purchase them. They use minimalistic photography to showcase their designs, however the main draw is to create items that are not offered anywhere else. May this be the design itself, or simply the material usage which would allow the products to be unique and desired. The website is easy to navigate and makes the commerce experience straightforward and aims to be as smooth as possible.

For the typefaces, Neue Montreal Mono from Pangram Pangram suited the clean and minimal aesthetic well at h1 and h2. Mono space typefaces are easy to read and fit the "Form and Function" styling. as for body copy, Pangran Pangram's Supply Sans is blocky and industrial which pairs well with the clean bold lines of Neue Montreal Mono.

Going into usability, System fonts should be used if the above mentioned typefaces are not available or cannot be injected in the markup. Choosing from the list of system typefaces available, Impact for h1, Tahoma for h2 and Courier New for body copy are the most similar to the typefaecs above. Though not a perfect match, they still have similar qualities and funtion the same roles.

Thoughtfully designed products are what make the company unique. To fit that, a palatte that allows for the products to speak for themselves is used. A white background helps to contrast with dark environments the customer may be in allowing for higher visibility and contrast. It also allows for the product to stand out against the stark white. Dividers are in a grey, not black as not to have too drastic of a change and ease the customer into the interface, rather than being uncomfortable. Finally, the bright blue accent allows for colour to be introduced as to provide more contrast between both the products, white space, and the dividing colour. The blue is similar to the blue used on blueprints harkoning back to the industrial style of the company.
